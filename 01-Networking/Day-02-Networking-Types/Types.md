
# 🌐 Types of Computer Networks (LAN, MAN, WAN)

## 📌 Introduction
Computer networks are categorized based on their size, coverage area, and purpose. The three main types of networks are LAN, MAN, and WAN.

---

## 🏠 1. LAN (Local Area Network)

### 📖 Definition:
A Local Area Network (LAN) connects devices within a small geographical area such as a home, office, or school.

### 🔑 Key Features:
- Limited to a small area  
- High speed and low latency  
- Privately owned and managed  
- Commonly uses Ethernet or Wi-Fi  

### 💡 Example:
Home Wi-Fi network connecting mobile phones, laptops, and printers.

---

## 🏙 2. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

### 📖 Definition:
A Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) covers a larger geographical area such as a city or large campus.

### 🔑 Key Features:
- Larger than LAN but smaller than WAN  
- Connects multiple LANs within a city  
- High-speed connectivity  
- Usually managed by organizations or service providers  

### 💡 Example:
City-wide internet services or university campus networks.

---

## 🌍 3. WAN (Wide Area Network)

### 📖 Definition:
A Wide Area Network (WAN) spans a large geographical area such as countries or continents.

### 🔑 Key Features:
- Covers large distances  
- Connects multiple LANs and MANs  
- Uses public communication links  
- Lower speed compared to LAN  

### 💡 Example:
The Internet is the largest WAN in the world.

---

## 📊 Comparison Table

| Feature        | LAN                | MAN                 | WAN                  |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Coverage Area | Small             | City                | Global               |
| Speed         | High              | Medium to High      | Lower than LAN       |
| Ownership     | Private           | Organization/ISP    | Public/Private       |

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
LAN, MAN, and WAN are essential types of networks that differ based on their size and usage. Understanding these helps in building a strong foundation in networking and cybersecurity.
